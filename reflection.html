Responsive Web Design or RWD allows a website to optimize itself to fit the screen of a device that it is being viewed through. Be it a mobile device, a tab or a tv screen, this allows the viewing experience to appear seamless and fluid. The way in which they function is accomplished by components known as "breakpoints". The layout of a website is recognized by the designs implemented both before a specific breakpoint and after it. It also employs a variety of techniques such as the ones mentioned below;

  • Fluid grid (page elements are sized by a percentage and not absolutes like pixels)
  • Flexible images
  • Allowing a page to use different CSS styles
  • Responsive layouts to adjust to screen sizes the page is being viewed on


    • Responsive Web Design relates to web design in one major way; the growth of mobile users. Now more that ever people are accessing websites through the dimensions of a phone which demands web pages to be dynamic in their ability to adapt to the form factors. Even more so phones come in many shapes and sizes therefore its paramount that a web site is versatile enough to cater to all its users equally. Adjusting screen resolution, Flexible images by the form of aligning an image according to a header and setting max widths to certain images so that they may not lose its quality in the process is pivotal for RWD.
